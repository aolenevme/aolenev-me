I have just finished reading the 1st part of a lovely book on OOP.

It was pleasant to see how the author's desire to increase the maintainability of a codebase almost entirely confirms Robert Martin's statement: O.f() === f(O);.

If the following principles sound awkward to you:

  1. No null
  2. No code in constructors
  3. No getters and setters
  4. No mutable objects
  5. No readers, parsers, controllers, sorters, and so on
  6. No static methods, not even private ones
  7. No instanceof, type casting, or reflection
  8. No public methods without a contract (interface)
  9. No statements in test methods except assertThat
  10. No ORM or ActiveRecord
  11. No implementation inheritance
