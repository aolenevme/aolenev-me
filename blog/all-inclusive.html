Businesses are run to make money.

And geeks usually forget about it because our industry is bloated with helicopter money.

But, in order to be profitable, we have to control costs. One of the main way to do so is by hiring "right" people. A common culture eases communication. More than anything else. But, what do we see now in IT?

There is deficit of qualified employees on local markets. To compensate, people are hired from all over the globe.

It is inefficient from management perspectives. Sometimes coders can barely understand each other.

It goes against the promoted Agile principles where an ideal team consists of people of same ages with synced schedules and etc.

Draw conclusions on your own.
