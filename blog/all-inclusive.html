Businesses are run to make money.

And geeks usually forget about it because our industry is bloated with helicopter money.

But, to be profitable, we have to control costs. One of the top ways to do so is by hiring the "right" people. A common culture eases communication. More than anything else. But what do we see now in IT?

There is a deficit of qualified employees in local markets. People are hired from all over the globe to compensate for it.

It is inefficient from the management's perspective. Sometimes coders can barely understand each other.

It goes against the promoted Agile principles where an ideal team consists of people of the same ages with synced schedules, etc.

Conclude on your own.
